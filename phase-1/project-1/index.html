About the Legend

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian Scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering.

Achievements

  • Played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development

  • Received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna India's highest civilian honor

  • Authored several books on science, technology, and spirituality, including Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds

Legacy

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Sir was known for his humility, wisdom, and dedication to the betterment of India and its people. He inspired generations of students and young people to pursue their dreams and contribute to society.
